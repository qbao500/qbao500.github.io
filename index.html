Simply, Bao

A self-driven student with a passion for programming and game development.

I'm a third-year Game Development student at Kajaani University of Applied Sciences (KAMK) in Finland. I'm currently looking for internship opportunities (Spring semester 2021), not only to apply my skills to more practical works, but also to develop them and acquire more experience.

I have sufficient experience with both Unity and Unreal Engine. However, I'm more confident and proficient in Unity with C#, rather than Unreal in which I use more Blueprint than C++ for my projects.       Combat - Atari 2600 (2019)

      This is actually an assignment in my Game Design course, in which the task is to replicate a very old game and "upgrade" it. I found this game on Retro Games page, which was made in 1977. It is a local multiplayer game that 2 players try to kill each other and compete who has more kills.

      The interesting thing for me is the ability to control the bullet while it's flying, although it's pretty hard and conflicting with your movement. However, it makes the game really fun and also requires some skill to be able to defeat the opponent. Thus, I replicated it similarly, and also implemented "teleport" mechanic, that allows player to teleport to the bullet.

      The game has no proper UI or sound, and no publishment, since I just developed it when I have freetime, until the deadline. However, a playtest was conducted and I found most of testers were enjoyable when playing an old-school local multiplayer game. I can feel the potential of this project and hopefully, I will able to continue developing this when the era of local multiplayer is back with new technologies.
